Kalpana Chawla

An Astronaut Who Touched the Stars

About Her

Kalpana Chawla was the first woman of Indian origin to go to space. She was born in Karnal, India, and became a naturalized U.S. citizen. As a NASA astronaut and aerospace engineer, she flew on two space missions. Her life was tragically cut short during the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster in 2003, but her legacy continues to inspire countless young minds around the world.

Key Achievements

  • First woman of Indian origin in space
  • Completed two space missions with NASA
  • Awarded the Congressional Space Medal of Honor posthumously
  • Inspiration to millions of aspiring scientists and explorers
